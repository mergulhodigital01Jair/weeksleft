Lived This week Ahead

How many weeks have I been alive?

The tool above counts the exact number of complete weeks between your date of birth and today, then maps every week of a full life onto a single grid. It updates the moment you change your birthday, and all of the maths runs entirely in your browser — nothing you type is stored or sent anywhere.

Why weeks, not years?

Years feel abstract and days feel overwhelming, but a week is a unit you actually live through — a Monday to Sunday you can recall. Seeing all of them at once is a useful kind of sobering. The filled squares are not a warning: they are proof of what you have already done. The empty ones are not a threat: they are an invitation.

Is the estimate accurate?

The "weeks remaining" figure is a simple calculation: life expectancy minus your current age, converted to weeks. Nobody knows their real number. That ambiguity is precisely the point — treat it as a prompt to spend the remaining squares intentionally, not as a prophecy about when the grid ends.

Download your poster

After generating your grid, click "Download poster" to save a clean PNG of your personal life chart. People frame it, use it as a desktop wallpaper, or share it as a reminder that a life is short enough to see at once — and long enough to do something meaningful with.
